Imran Khan

Pakistan Cricket Legend & World Cup Winning Captain

Career Information

Imran Khan is one of the greatest cricketers in the history of Pakistan. He captained Pakistan to its first-ever ICC Cricket World Cup victory in 1992. He was known for his leadership, fast bowling, and all-round performance.

Major Achievements

1992 World Cup Victory: Captained Pakistan to their sole ODI World Cup title, acting as a pivotal leader in the final.
All-Rounder’s Triple: Achieved the elite feat of scoring over 3,000 runs and taking over 300 wickets in just 75 Test matches.
Legendary Bowling Records: Finished his Test career with 362 wickets and 182 ODI wickets. He holds the record for most wickets as a captain in Test cricket.
Incredible All-Rounder Peak: In his last 10 years of Test cricket, he averaged 19.16 with the ball (better than contemporaries like Malcolm Marshall) and 51.34 with the bat.
Captaincy & Leadership: Credited for transforming Pakistan into a premier Test-playing nation, achieving rare series wins in England and India.
Awards: Inducted into the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame in 2010, Wisden Cricketer of the Year (1983), and International Cricketer of the Year (1989).
